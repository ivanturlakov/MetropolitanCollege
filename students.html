Crimonology

Best described as the study of making the law, breaking the law and society’s reaction to breaking the law. Criminology degrees lead toward careers as Special Agents for the FBI, DEA, ATF, Juvenile Probation Officer, Caseworkers, Drug, and Alcohol Abuse Counselor, and Crime Analyst.

English as a Second Language Caminos Hacia el Exito at JCSU- Johnson C. Smith University’s Metropolitan College presents a special program: English as a Second Language. This certificate program teaches speakers of other languages essential English skills while giving students a challenging opportunity to be successful while addressing the student’s individual learning styles.

The course focuses on listening, reading, speaking, grammar, and enunciation skills. With the assistance of an English as a Second Language certified instructor, each student is afforded the opportunity to read with understanding, convey ideas in writing, learn basic skills, and interpret and communicate through oral presentations, group participation, and one-on-one personalized care. The class, which lasts eight weeks, meets one night a week for two hours. There are three levels: beginning, intermediate, and advanced. There is sure to be one course offering that is just right for you! For more information, contact the Metropolitan College’s Cheryl Curtis, Director of Educational Enhancement Services at 704-378-3581.

Social Work

Learn a range of skills and methods to help people: child service, clinical, therapeutic services, and community development. Social work leads to a variety of careers from advocacy organizations and healthcare to government and corporate human relations.

The Social Work program provides an inclusive learning environment that demonstrates respect for the diversity of students and staff by providing opportunities to engage in a variety of programs and community service initiatives through the University, Social Work Club, the National Association of Black Social Workers, the National Association of Christians in Social Work and the community-at-large; attending workshops and conferences; opportunities to serve on the Department’s Community Advisory Board as well as the Department hosting an information session each semester to obtain information from students and staff on a variety of departmental matters. Our Field Education program continues to identify various settings that reflect the differences and diversity of our student’s interest. Additionally, students engage in a variety of service learning activities that extend outside of the classroom. The diversity and respect for all is also demonstrated in the program’s efforts to be inclusive of persons with disabilities. The institutional disability policy is included on every course syllabi and verbally discussed at the start of every course. Students are directed towards the universities Office of Disabilities Services and encouraged to establish the appropriate academic plan to ensure that any and all necessary accommodations are offered to increase chances of academic success.

Sport Management

Learn sport finance and sport public relations that emphasize real world knowledge that you can apply to a variety of career paths that include: sport finance, sport marketing, sport law, pro-team administration, and college team administration.

The JCSU Online Sport Management program will enable sport enthusiasts from across the nation to benefit from the array of sport opportunities offered in Charlotte, NC by professional sport teams including: NBA, NFL, and minor league sport franchises, as well as private and public sport organizations. Our program will grant students the opportunity to “PLAY to WIN” by obtaining invaluable practical experiences working in the industry in various sport marketing, event management, facilities management, ticket sales capacities, etc. with events such as: the CIAA Basketball Tournament, the NCAA Regional Basketball Tournament, the Dr. Pepper ACC Football Championship, just to name a few. The Sport Management curriculum consists of a total of 122 credit hours. Of this total, 54 credit hours are designed to provide students with a wealth of theoretical knowledge in various content areas relevant to the sport industry including, but not limited to: Sport Marketing, Sport Law, Sport Facility and Event Management, Sport Sociology and Ethics, and Sport Psychology. A maximum number of 90 credit hours can be transferred into the program. Each student enrolled in JCSU’s Online Sport Management program is also required to complete two courses as a part of the curriculum that will enable him or her to ascertain practical experiences in the sport industry: SPM 233 – Sport Practicum and SPM 433 – Sport Internship. The SPM 233 course is a prerequisite to SPM 433 and is designed to provide students with a mentoring/shadowing experience with sport professionals in his or her local area. The student will be required to obtain 60 hours of field experiences through this course. The SPM 433 course will enable the student to obtain extensive practical experiences working in a sport setting. The student will be required to complete 120 hours of field experiences in a sport setting of his or her choice. A special feature of JCSU’s Online Sport Management program is that students who wish to complete their practicum or internship experiences in the Charlotte, NC area, may do so in either or both of these courses
